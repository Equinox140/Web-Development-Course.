Classic Vanilla Butter Cake Recipe.

Ingredients You'll Need

  • 2 ¼ cups (270g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 ½ cups (300g) granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (240ml) whole milk, room temperature

Step-By-Step Instructions.

  1. Prep your oven and pans.
    Prep your oven and pans
    Grease two 9-inch round cake pans and line the bottoms with parchment paper. This prevents the cake from sticking.

  2. Whisk the dry ingredients
    2 minutes
    In a medium bowl, vigorously whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Setting this aside ensures the leavening agent is evenly distributed.

  3. Cream the butter and sugar
    3 to 5 minutes
    Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together on medium-high speed until the mixture looks pale, fluffy, and doubled in volume. Don't rush this step—this incorporates the air needed for a light cake.

  4. Add eggs and vanilla
    2 minutes
    Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the vanilla extract. Tip: If the mixture looks slightly curdled here, it's fine, it will come together.

  5. Alternate dry and wet ingredients
    Combine gently
    With the mixer on low speed, add 1/3 of the flour mixture, followed by 1/2 of the milk. Repeat, and finish with the final 1/3 of the flour. Mix just until combined. Overmixing at this stage activates the gluten and makes the cake dense.

  6. Bake and test
    22 to 26 minutes
    Divide the batter evenly between your two pans. Bake for 22–26 minutes. Test readiness by inserting a toothpick into the center—it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs, but no wet batter.

The Golden Rule of Baking: Make sure your eggs, butter, and milk are truly at room temperature. Cold ingredients won't emulsify properly, which causes the batter to trap less air and results in a heavy, flat cake.
